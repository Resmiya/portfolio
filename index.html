Who is Steve Jobs?

Steve Jobs was an American business magnate, industrial designer, investor, and media proprietor. He served as the chairman, chief executive officer (CEO), and co-founder of Apple Inc., chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar, a member of the board of directors of The Walt Disney Company following the acquisition of Pixar, and founder, chairman, and CEO of NeXT. Jobs is widely recognized as a pioneer of the personal computer revolution of the 1970s and 1980s, along with Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak.

Steve Jobs Timeline:

  • 1976

    1976 One of the co-founders of Apple Computer along with Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. In the same year, the company sold the Apple I in kit form for $666.66.

  • 5 June 1977

    Released the Apple II, the first commercially successful personal computer housed in plastic with color graphics - and Apple's first successful personal computer.

  • 24 January 1984

    Two days after the $1.5 million Ridley Scott-directed Super Bowl ad "1984," introduced the Macintosh to much fanfare during an Apple shareholders' meeting. "For the first time ever, I want Macintosh to speak for itself." The computer's voice then said, "Never trust a computer you can't lift." The Macintosh became the first commercially successful small computer with a graphical user interface.

  • 12 September 1985

    CEO John Sculley engineered Jobs' ousting from Apple. Jobs resigned as head of Apple, saying at a board meeting, "I've been thinking a lot, and it's time for me to move on. Clearly, I must do something. I'm 30 years old..." Shortly after, Jobs started NeXT Computer (which later became NeXT Software), funded by selling $70 million worth of his Apple stock. A NeXT "interpersonal" workstation, using built-in Ethernet ports, was used by Tim Berners-Lee at CERN to be the first server of the World Wide Web.

  • 3 February 1986

    Acquired the Graphics Group division from Lucasfilm for $10 million, which became Pixar Animation Studios.

  • 10 December 1996

    Returned to Apple, as an advisor, after buying NeXT for $429 million.

  • 10 November 1997

    Introduced the Apple Store, allowing consumers to order Apple products directly from the company online.

  • 23 October 2001

    Introduced the iPod:

    "With iPod, Apple has invented a whole new category of digital music player that lets you put your entire music collection in your pocket and listen to it wherever you go...With iPod, listening to music will never be the same again."

    Apple mengirimkan lebih dari 300 juta iPod pada Oktober 2011.

  • 2 February 2006

    iTunes sold its billionth song:

    "I hope that every customer, artist and music company executive takes a moment today to reflect on what we've achieved together during the past three years."

    Apple mengirimkan lebih dari 300 juta iPod pada Oktober 2011.

  • 9 January 2007

    Dropped "Computer" from the Apple name. Introduced the iPhone:

    "iPhone is a revolutionary and magical product that is literally five years ahead of any other mobile phone...We are all born with the ultimate pointing device--our fingers--and iPhone uses them to create the most revolutionary user interface since the mouse."

    Apple menguasai 19,1 persen pasar ponsel pintar per Agustus 2011, setelah mengapalkan 108 juta iPhone ke seluruh dunia pada Maret 2011.

  • 15 January 2008

    Introduced the App Store as an update to iTunes. Also introduced the MacBook Air. The App Store facilitated 18 billion app downloads by October 4, 2011.

  • 27 January 2010

    Introduced the iPad. Apple sold 500,000 iPads during their first week on the market and captured 84% of the tablet market by the end of the year, with an estimated 12.9 million shipped by December 10, 2010, and 25 million shipped worldwide by June 2011. That same month, the company revealed that they had sold 200 million iOS devices.

  • 5 October 2011

    He died of respiratory arrest related to the tumor in 2011, and in 2022, was posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

"For the past 33 years, I have looked in the mirror every morning and asked myself: 'If today were the last day of my life, would I want to do what I am about to do today?' And whenever the answer has been 'No' for too many days in a row, I know I need to change something."

Steve Jobs
